Racialized Organizations and Decolonial Possibilities

I am a PhD candidate in Business Administration at FGV/EAESP, with a research focus in Organizational Studies. I hold a Master’s degree in Administration from CEFET/MG, specializing in decision-making processes in organizational settings. I earned my Bachelor's degree in Business Administration from the Federal University of Juiz de Fora and hold a postgraduate certificate in People Management from UEMG.

I am a member of the Organizational Studies, Society and Subjectivity Research Group (NOSS). I have worked as a Teaching Assistant in Health Management courses at FGV and as a Research Assistant at GVPesquisa. With 16 years of experience in public sector management, my academic interests include racial relations in organizations, decolonial management, Indigenous and Quilombola organizing, and historical inequalities.

Research

My research explores the intersections of racialized organizations, decolonial approaches, and alternative epistemologies. I investigate how Quilombola and Indigenous communities organize and resist dominant managerial logics through culturally rooted practices and worldviews. My doctoral project analyzes the political, social, and epistemic dynamics of these organizations and their contributions to management and organizational theory.
